3. Gummo (1997).

When I first watched this film I honestly had no idea what it was supposed to mean. Movie subjects the people of a small town trying to make meaning in their unfulfilled, boring lives.
The people are isolated from the outside world and revolve around playing, it is really interesting to see the world from this kind of perspective. And after all, does a film has to have a point?

5. Taxidermia (2006).

This is an exploration of the bizarre of three generations of men, including a pervert of new satisfactions, an obese eater and a passionate embalmer.
It's totally well-shot and well-acted, especially Marc Bischoff. I liked the plot and I thought it was unique, even though disgusting in some parts. What I find it weird about this film is that it is supposed to be a representation of the political history of Hungary.
